**Vocabulary drift — reconciliation recommendation.**

The `blocker_class` values used in initiatives (this review's Part A header schema) are **different terms than** the `gap_type` values in `gap_types.yaml` / `data_gaps` sub-entries. This is a real schema drift:

| blocker_class (initiative-level) | closest gap_type (data_gaps-level) |
|---|---|
| `data_source_missing` | `missing_source_system` |
| `schema_group_missing` | `missing_conformed_entity` or `missing_conformed_layer` or `missing_history` (depends on what's missing) |
| `tool_missing` | `tool_missing` ✅ already aligned |
| `primitive_missing` | *no direct equivalent* — closest are `insufficient_outcome_labels`, `missing_event_timeline` (specific instances) |
| `governance_missing` | `missing_conformed_layer` (when the "governance" IS the mapping layer) |

**Proposed reconciliation: treat `gap_types.yaml` as canonical and align `blocker_class` vocabulary to it.** The gap_types list is more specific and already load-bearing in multiple data_gaps sub-entries. Concretely:

1. Rename `blocker_class: data_source_missing` → `missing_source_system` across initiatives. Keep gap_types.yaml unchanged.
2. Replace `blocker_class: schema_group_missing` with the specific gap_type (`missing_conformed_entity` / `missing_conformed_layer` / `missing_history`) that applies per initiative. E.g. loss_ratio_forecasting becomes `missing_history`; broker_performance_intelligence becomes `weak_identifier` (which is already in gap_types.yaml and actually fits better).
