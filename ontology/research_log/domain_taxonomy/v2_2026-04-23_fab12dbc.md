## Domain definitions

### distribution
- **Scope:** Channel identity and economics — brokers, coverholders, MGAs, binders, line-slips, delegated authority, commission paid to distributors.
- **Boundary:** Commission *as a distribution economic* belongs here; commission *as a rating-model input* (the `commission` column on `do_layer_pricing`) double-matches pricing and should probably primary-match pricing in this warehouse.
- **Warehouse artefacts:** `*_commission_details`, `*_summary_brokerage`, columns like `commission`, `brokerage`, `expiring_commission_percentage`. Very thin signal overall — consistent with the flagged "distribution gap".

### underwriting
- **Scope:** Quote lifecycle, underwriter identity, risk selection, coverage terms, bind decision.
- **Boundary vs pricing:** The `quote_*` asset family genuinely double-matches. Rule of thumb: tables primarily describing *the risk and its terms* (`quote_setup`, `quote_policy_detail`, `quote_coverage_detail`, `*_quote`) are underwriting; tables computing *a price* (`layer_pricing`, `rating_details`, `adjusted_premium`, `rate_monitoring`) are pricing.
- **Warehouse artefacts:** `quote_setup`, `quote_coverage_detail`, `quote_policy_detail`, `*_quote`, `do_quote`, `hx_do_rating_factors` (ambiguous — see open questions), `uw_adjustment`, `uw_comment`, `new_renewal`, `policy_is_quoted`, `coverage`, `limit`, `excess`, `inception_date`, `expiry_date`.

### pricing
- **Scope:** Technical rate-making — technical/modified-technical premium, ELR/ELC, RARC, rate change, layer pricing, loadings/discounts, pricing adequacy.
- **Boundary vs profitability:** `tech_gnwp`, `modtech_gnwp` are pricing when used in rate-monitoring / adequacy (`rate_monitoring`, `layer_pricing`); they are profitability when used in `profitability_measures` / `sold_to_plan` attribution. This matches the reference's explicit note.
- **Warehouse artefacts:** `*_layer_pricing*`, `*_rating*`, `rate_monitoring*`, `hx_fi_adjusted_premium`, `*_elr*`, `*_modifiers`, columns: `tech_ggwp`, `modtech_ggwp`, `tech_elc`, `modtech_elc`, `tech_gg_elr`, `rate_change`, `gross_rarc`, `net_rarc`, `base_rate`, `loadings`, `burn_rate_*`, `gglr_*`, `gnlr_*`, `loss_cost_inflation`, `trend_index`, `dev_pattern`.

### exposure_management
- **Scope:** Aggregation, concentration, catastrophe modelling, zonal/geographic exposure, PML.
- **Boundary:** Exposure *measures on a single risk* used as pricing inputs (`exposure_measure`, `claims_exposure`) are ambiguous; in this warehouse they are almost always pricing inputs, not portfolio aggregations. Keep `exposure_management` lean; don't over-grab.
- **Warehouse artefacts:** `*_exposure_model`, `*_regional_exposure*`, `contingency_catastrophe`, `hx_fi_exposure_info`, columns `gross_exposure_change`, `country`, `main_domicile`.

### operations
- **Scope:** Endorsements, MTAs, cancellations, renewal *processing*, in-force servicing.
- **Warehouse signal:** **Very weak.** `core_policy_view`, `tbl_Policy_and_Claim_CorePolicyView`, `tbl_Policy_and_Claim_CoreSchemeView` are the only candidates; they may equally be underwriting data. Expect near-zero assets to primary-match operations. Retain the domain for completeness, but flag the gap.

### claims
- **Scope (narrowed for this warehouse):** Loss history used for experience rating, specific losses, claims activity fed into pricing. True FNOL / adjuster / case-reserve lifecycle is probably *not present* in the warehouse.
- **Boundary:** `gglr_incurred` etc. on a pricing table is pricing (loss-ratio-as-rate-input); `claims_experience`, `specific_losses`, `claims_details` asset-level are claims.
- **Warehouse artefacts:** `claims_experience*`, `*_specific_losses`, `*_claims_details*`, `eupi_claims_*`, `hx_fi_claims_activity`, `do_entity_claims_history`, columns `incurred`, `paid`, `claims_inflation`.

### reserving
- **Scope:** IBNR, ultimate loss, LDFs, BF/Chain-Ladder, reserve adequacy.
- **Warehouse signal:** **Weak.** Columns `gglr_ultimate`, `gnlr_ultimate`, `burn_rate_ultimate`, `dev_pattern` hint at actuarial-ultimate methodology, but these sit inside pricing tables as rating inputs. Likely zero assets primary-match reserving.

### reinsurance
- **Scope:** Inward/outward, treaty, facultative, recoveries, retro.
- **Warehouse signal:** **Essentially absent** in sampled names. `our_share_pct`, `our_share_pct_london`, `london_order_percentage`, `london_estimated_signed_line` relate to Lloyd's co-insurance shares/subscription — *not* reinsurance in the treaty-economics sense. Do **not** keyword on `share` for reinsurance; it will false-positive on every quote.

### profitability
- **Scope:** Result attribution — sold-to-plan, sold-to-tech, sold-to-modtech, target-to-plan, combined/loss ratio for reporting, GGWP/GNWP at sold level for P&L view.
- **Boundary vs pricing:** See overlap note above. Strongest profitability signal: asset name contains `profitability_measures` or `ra_financial*`, or column family `sold_to_*`, `target_to_*`, `change_in_sold_to_*`.
- **Warehouse artefacts:** `profitability_measures*`, `ra_financials`, `ra_financial_metrics`, `ra_financial_scoring`, `hx_do_financials`, `hx_fi_premium_summary`, columns `sold_ggwp`, `sold_gnwp`, `sold_to_tech`, `sold_to_modtech`, `target_to_plan`, `target_gg_elr`.

### portfolio_monitoring
- **Scope:** Cross-book drift, KPI roll-ups, rate-change monitoring at portfolio level, expiring-vs-renewing comparisons.
- **Boundary:** `rate_monitoring` is ambiguous — the name suggests monitoring, but the column set (`tech_gnwp`, `modtech_gnwp`, single-risk layer pricing) suggests pricing adequacy per quote. **Recommend primary-matching `rate_monitoring*` to pricing**, keep portfolio_monitoring for true roll-up/dashboard assets (of which the warehouse has few).
- **Warehouse artefacts:** `expiring_*` column family, `breadth_of_cover_change`, `policy_term_change`, `other_changes`, `gross_limits_and_excess_change`, `gross_exposure_change`.

### compliance
- **Scope:** Sanctions, conduct, SII QRTs, GDPR tags.
- **Warehouse signal:** **Absent** in the sample. Retain the domain but expect zero matches.

### data_quality *(cross-cutting, new)*
- **Scope:** Pipeline plumbing — rejected rows, landing/raw tiers, ingestion metadata.
- **Warehouse artefacts:** `bad_records`, `hx_landing`, `hx_raw`, `dp_landing`, `dp_raw`, columns `_pdm_last_update_timestamp`, `ingestion_timestamp`.
- **Rationale:** Without this, `landing`/`raw` assets silently drop out of every domain and skew "unassigned" counts; with it, curators can filter them deliberately.

## Open questions for the curator

1. **Rename `profitability` → `finance_profitability`?** The reference groups finance-accounting and profitability together. Pro: faithful to reference, admits future GL/earning assets. Con: key change breaks existing downstream consumers and mis-signals scope given the warehouse has no GL artefacts today. **Recommendation: keep `profitability` key, document that finance-accounting assets are out-of-scope for this warehouse.**

2. **`rate_monitoring*` assets — pricing or portfolio_monitoring?** The name says monitoring; the column set (`tech_gnwp`, `modtech_gnwp`, `sold_to_tech`, per-quote rows) says pricing adequacy. Recommended primary = pricing, secondary = portfolio_monitoring. Curator should confirm the business intent: is this table consumed by UW for per-risk decisions, or by portfolio teams for book-level drift?

3. **`contingency_catastrophe`** — is this a cat-exposure artefact (exposure_management) or a class-of-business peril schedule within contingency underwriting (underwriting)? Recommendation: underwriting, but requires a look at the table's columns.

4. **`our_share_pct`, `our_share_pct_london`, `london_order_percentage`, `london_estimated_signed_line`** — confirm these are Lloyd's co-insurance / subscription-market shares, not reinsurance cessions. If any of them are actually ceded-share fields, reinsurance keywords need `share` added.
