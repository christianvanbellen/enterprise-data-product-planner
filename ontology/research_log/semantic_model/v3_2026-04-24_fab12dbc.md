### Entity definitions (new/changed only)

**`claim_development`** — the actuarial time-series projection of a claim cohort. Boundary: a `claim` is adjuster-managed and point-in-time; `claim_development` is a (yoa_cohort × evaluation_date) snapshot series for chain-ladder / BF projection. Expected artefacts: triangle tables keyed by `(yoa, development_month)`, columns `gglr_incurred`, `gnlr_incurred`, `burn_rate_*` at successive evaluation dates. Today the warehouse holds these at latest evaluation only; the entity is the shape the data will take when cohort snapshotting lands.

**`submission`** — the pre-bind artefact (broker email, ACORD form, placement-platform record) that precedes a `quote`. Boundary: `quote` begins once the submission is keyed into the PAS; `submission` is everything before that. Expected artefacts: submission_id, submission_received_date, broker_id, document_ref, status (new / declined / quoted).

**`cat_event`** — a modelled or actual catastrophe scenario with a portfolio loss profile. Boundary: `claim` is realised loss; `cat_event` is a modelled/hypothetical event carrying PML, event_id, RP, peril. Expected artefacts: event_set tables from RMS/AIR/Verisk, RDS scenarios.

**`reinsurance_treaty`** — an outward (or inward) treaty/facultative placement. Boundary: distinct from `policy` (gross of RI) and from `profitability_component` (net-of-RI aggregates). Expected artefacts: treaty_id, layer/share, cession terms, reinstatement terms, recoverables.

**`bordereau`** — a coverholder's risk schedule under a binding authority. Boundary: distinct from `policy` (direct-placed) and from the `broker` entity (which identifies the party, not the schedule). Expected artefacts: umr, bordereau_id, bordereau_period, risk-level rows conforming to Lloyd's Coverholder Reporting Standards.

**`endorsement`** — an in-force policy change (MTA, cancellation, mid-term adjustment). Boundary: the `policy` carries the in-force state; `endorsement` is the event stream of changes to it. Expected artefacts: endorsement_id, endorsement_effective_date, change_type, premium_impact.

**`regulatory_return`** — a regulatory reporting artefact (SII QRT template, Lloyd's return). Boundary: the measures INSIDE a return live under their value-chain entity (`pricing_component`, `profitability_component`); the return itself is a governance artefact tracking submission, version, signoff.

## Part F — Open questions

1. **`claim_development` as a standalone entity vs subtype of `claim`?** I proposed a new entity because reserving triangles are a structurally different shape (cohort × evaluation_date matrix) from a point-in-time claim record. But an alternative is to keep `claim` and register `claims_development_triangle` primitive with `required_entities: [claim]` + aspirational columns on `claim`'s signature. The latter is lighter-weight; the former makes the gap more visible. **Curator decision.**

2. **`our_share_pct_*` columns on which entity signature?** The `subscription_share` primitive points at them but they don't sit cleanly on `pricing_component` (they're distribution-shaped) or `profitability_component` (they're inputs to, not outputs). Options: (a) add to `pricing_component` signature; (b) create a `subscription_position` entity; (c) leave as global columns without an entity binding and rely on metric_patterns to tag them. **Leaning (c)** — these are measures, not entity-definitional columns.

3. **`reinsurance_treaty` vs future inward/assumed distinction.** The initiative is scoped to outward only. If inward reinsurance writing is material to the book, the entity may need to split or carry a `treaty_direction` tag. **Curator confirm** with the reinsurance team.

4. **`submission` vs `quote` boundary.** Some placement platforms push `submission_id` and `quote_id` as the same artefact at different lifecycle stages; others keep them distinct. The proposal treats them as distinct entities. **Curator to verify** with the UW ops team when placement-platform ingestion is being scoped.

5. **`bordereau` overlap with `policy`.** A bordereau line IS a policy under a binding authority. Is `bordereau` a distinct entity or a `policy` + source-system tag? Proposed as distinct because the governance (Lloyd's Coverholder Reporting Standards), cadence (bordereaux are periodic schedules), and granularity (risk-schedule-level, often below policy_id) differ.

6. **Borderline metric_pattern keys flagged for post-audit review** (`rate_index`, `order_pct`, `lsm_share`, `cat_ap`, `loading_discount`, `relativity_value`, `lead_fee`, `hl_brokerage`). The brief recommends retain-until-proven-dead. A next audit report showing zero matches would justify removal.

7. **gap_types rename `missing_source_system` → `data_source_missing`**. Clean semantic canonicalisation but touches the `gap_type:` strings inside `data_gaps:` blocks across initiative_research.yaml. **Curator decision** on whether to rename now (coordinated commit) or defer. The brief recommends rename.

8. **gap_types additions `schema_group_missing`, `governance_missing`, `primitive_missing`**. All three appear warranted; all three are distinct from existing entries. **Curator may prefer to merge** `schema_group_missing` into `missing_conformed_entity` if the granularity distinction is not load-bearing in practice.

9. **Reference framework is synthesised, not authoritative.** Every recommendation above that leans on specific Lloyd's / CAS / IFoA terminology (especially reserving triangles, RDS, subscription-market mechanics) should be validated against a real internal reference before the curator freezes vocabulary. The framework itself flags this at the top of the file.
