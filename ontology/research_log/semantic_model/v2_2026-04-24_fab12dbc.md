### Entity definitions (new/materially changed only)

**`expiring_snapshot`** — Prior-period (expiring) values of premium, rate, limit, deductible, exposure, and commission held alongside the current quote/renewal for rate-change decomposition. Boundary: unlike `pricing_component` (current technical), this entity is *prior-period-tagged* and appears on renewal-style assets. Typical artefacts: `do_expiring_inputs`, `do_expiring_layers`, `hx_fi_expiring_info`, `hx_fi_expiring_layer`. Column family: `expiring_*`.

**`rate_monitoring_snapshot`** — Portfolio/roll-up view of rate-change and share metrics, distinct from per-quote pricing. Boundary: `rate_change` on `do_quote` is pricing; `rate_change` on `rate_monitoring` or `RMCoverageLayerView` is monitoring. Typical artefacts: `rate_monitoring`, `rate_monitoring_total_our_share_usd`, `hx_rate_monitoring*`, `ll_rate_monitoring*`, `RMCoverageLayerView`, `RMTotalsNewLSMSharesView`.

**`financial_scoring`** — Policyholder financial health inputs used in underwriting risk assessment (insured balance-sheet and ratio metrics). Boundary: `entity`/`policyholder_name` is `policyholder`; financial ratios are `financial_scoring`. Typical artefacts: `ra_financial_metrics`, `ra_financial_scoring`, `ra_financials`, `ra_inputs`, `hx_ra_*`.

**`participation`** — The carrier's share/line on a placement (written line, signed line, order %). Boundary: `limit`/`excess` is coverage; `our_share_pct` is how much of that coverage we hold. May be folded into `coverage` if curator prefers. Typical artefacts: `*_total_our_share_usd` view variants, `RMTotalsNewLSMSharesView`.

## Part E — Open questions

1. **`participation` as an entity or a measure family.** The carrier's share/line is either (a) a first-class entity (proposed above), or (b) a set of measures hanging off `coverage`. The warehouse signal supports either. **Curator decision.** Recommendation: start with entity for cleaner primitive modelling; fold later if it never carries discriminating weight.

2. **`quote` vs `policy` distinctness.** The reference treats them as adjacent; the warehouse keys off `quote_id` + `pas_id` with no standalone `policy_id`. Retain both conservatively, but if `policy`'s signature columns ever appear on the *same* assets as `quote`'s (likely), the two entities are effectively co-present on every fact and `policy` becomes redundant. Revisit after audit.

3. **`financial_scoring` naming.** Is this better called `insured_financials`, `policyholder_financials`, or `risk_assessment_input`? Reference framework doesn't have a named bucket (the closest is §2 Underwriting — "risk appetite assessment"). **Curator decision.**

4. **`rate_monitoring_snapshot` vs `pricing_component` on shared columns.** Columns like `rate_change`, `gross_rarc`, `net_rarc` appear on both per-quote pricing assets (`do_quote`) and roll-up monitoring assets (`rate_monitoring`). The proposed signatures put these on **both** entities. That's correct — the same *column name* can bind to different entities on different assets via signature scoring. Confirm this double-signature is intentional.

5. **`expiring_*` concept naming.** The metric patterns above map `expiring_ggwp` → `gross_written_premium` (same concept as `ggwp`). Alternative: introduce `expiring_gross_written_premium` as a distinct concept to keep period-tagging visible in the graph. Cleaner but more concepts. **Curator decision.**

6. **`underwriter` gap-aware retention.** Three audits in, still zero candidacy. Still worth keeping as a backlog signal, or is the point made? Recommendation: keep at least until the data team acknowledges the gap in writing.

7. **`claims_inflation` entity home.** This column appears on `rate_monitoring*` assets (as a rate-change driver) *and* arguably as a claims-development adjustment. Currently only in `rate_monitoring_snapshot` signature. Is there a claims-side use? Unclear from signal alone.

8. **Unmapped `loss_cost_inflation`.** Exists in warehouse, proposed to map to `claims_inflation` concept. Verify with curator that "loss cost inflation" (trend applied to historical losses) and "claims inflation" (current-period inflation load) are actually the same concept in this business. They may not be.

9. **Reference framework thinness.** The reference is explicit that it's a synthesis. Entities for Reserving (IBNR, LDFs), Reinsurance (treaties, ceded premium), and Compliance (sanctions, QRTs) are absent from both reference and warehouse. Not proposing entities for these — but they are real domain gaps, not taxonomy bugs. Flag for data-team roadmap.
