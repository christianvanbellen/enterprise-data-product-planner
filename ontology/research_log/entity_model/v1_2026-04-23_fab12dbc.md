# Entity & Primitive Model Review

**Caveat up front.** The reference framework is explicitly labelled a "synthesised reference, not a reproduction of any single authoritative source." Its domain boundaries are defensible and internally consistent, but the curator should not treat its sub-domain lines (e.g. pricing vs finance, claims vs reserving) as regulatory ground truth. Where I recommend entity splits below, I lean conservative — splitting only where the warehouse signal genuinely supports it.

The empirical baseline is stark: **72% of assets are unbound**, and the top unbound tokens (`tech`, `quote`, `summary`, `premium`, `hull`, `limit`, `rate`, `deductible`) are all domain-relevant — these are not junk assets, they are recognition failures. Most of the failure is explained by: (1) the `quote` concept not being a recognised entity despite being the single most common unbound token after `tech`, (2) pricing-component signatures missing `rate` and `premium` (marked dead, but those tokens appear ~90× in unbound assets — the dead signature is a false reading caused by normalisation, or they appear as `gross_premium` / `base_rate` / `tech_rate` etc. not the literal string), and (3) the `contingency_*` and `do_*` families having no asset-name-pattern hooks.

### Entity definitions

- **policy**: the bound contract. Warehouse artefacts: `core_policy_view`, `quote_policy_detail*`, `tbl_Policy_and_Claim_CorePolicyView`. Columns: `policy_*`, `inception_date`, `expiry_date`, `new_renewal`.
- **quote** (new): the pre-bind submission/pricing artefact. Boundary: everything keyed by `quote_id` that isn't yet a policy. In practice most warehouse rows carry `quote_id` even post-bind — so `quote` will co-occur with `policy` on many assets; that's fine (the multi-entity case is expected per the reference's overlap notes).
- **policyholder**: the insured. Boundary: identity and risk characteristics of the insured entity (not the broker, not the underwriter). Artefacts: `do_entity_attributes`, `eupi` entity fields.
- **broker**: distribution counterparty. Identity + commission %.
- **coverage**: the coverage section / sub-coverage priced within a policy. Artefacts: `CoverageView`, `CoverageSubCoverageView*`, `eupi_additional_coverage`, `quote_coverage_detail`.
- **layer** (new): an excess/limit band within a coverage, often separately priced. Artefacts: `*_layers`, `*_layer_pricing`, `ClaimLayersView`, `RMCoverageLayerView`, `contingency_layers`, `do_expiring_layers`, `hx_fi_layer_pricing`.
- **claim**: loss record. Artefacts: all `*_claims_*`, `*_claim_experience*`, `specific_losses`.
- **pricing_component**: technical rate-making artefacts (tech/modtech/sold premium, ELC, rate, RARC). Artefacts: `rate_monitoring`, `quote_premium_details`, `*_hl_*_premium`, `ra_financials`.
- **profitability_component**: result-attribution artefacts (sold-to-tech, sold-to-modtech, plan variances, commission-as-expense). Artefacts: `profitability_measures*`.
- **exposure**: the measure of at-risk units (sum insured, PML, exposure measure, headcount, fleet size). Artefacts: `claims_exposure`, `eupi_exposure_model`, `hx_fi_exposure_info`, `general_aviation_fleet`, `RegionalExposureView`.
- **underwriter** *(if retained)*: identity of the UW decision-maker. Currently dead — recommend removing until a UW-identity dimension lands.

## Part D — Open questions

1. **Is `quote` really distinct from `policy` in this warehouse?** Every policy signature column (`policy_id`, `policy_number`, `policy_term`) is dead, but `quote_id` / `policy_is_quoted` are ubiquitous. It's plausible this warehouse doesn't model a separate policy entity at all and `quote` *is* the unit of underwriting record. The curator should decide: (a) keep both `quote` and `policy` with `policy` thin and mostly carried by the conformed group, or (b) collapse to a single `quote` entity and retire `policy`. I've proposed (a) conservatively.

2. **Does `layer` cleanly sit under Underwriting, Exposure, or span both?** The reference §4 says exposure is portfolio aggregation; §2 underwriting owns line-size/share. A layer-limit is arguably both. I've proposed it as its own entity rather than forcing a domain. Curator may prefer to call it a sub-type of coverage.

3. **Are `exposure_type` and `coverage_type` the same underlying concept in this warehouse?** `exposure_type` is in the top columns, `coverage_type` is marked dead. Verify whether these were conflated during normalisation; if so, the coverage signature should use `exposure_type`.

4. **`underwriter` retention.** Is there a UW-identity asset I'm missing (e.g. an HR-adjacent table not in this bundle)? If yes, the entity should stay; if not, remove to reduce orphan noise.

5. **`limit_100` vs `limit` / `layer_limit`.** The current `exposure_structure` primitive requires `limit_100`, which I can't confirm is present — the top-columns list shows `limit` and `layer_limit` but not `limit_100`. Curator should verify whether `limit_100` was a pre-normalisation column name that's been renamed.

6. **Reinsurance, reserving, compliance as domains with no entities.** The reference framework has rich sections for all three. This bundle shows no clear asset families for any of them. Is that because they're genuinely out of scope for this warehouse, or because they're in a separate bundle? If separate, the entity model will need a future pass.

7. **`policy_totals` conformed group.** It maps to `policy` today. Is that the right call, or should it bind to a separate `policy_financials` entity that sits closer to `profitability_component`? The ambiguity is the one flagged in the reference under "Pricing vs Finance & profitability."

8. **Confidence weights.** The audit shows `profitability_component` entities landing at exactly 0.40 mean confidence — right at `min_threshold`. One row of evidence drift and they drop out. Worth reviewing whether `signal_2_scale: 0.8` is too punitive now that signatures are being tightened, or whether the threshold itself should be lowered to 0.35.
